Browser Extension Data Leak

10th July 2025

It's another day, another coffee spitting vulnerability on your user's machines. This one rides in their browsers and has been hidden in browser extensions that have been verified for years in App stores.

With the automatic update mechanisms for browser extensions, the malicious versions silently deployed to over 2.3 million users, turning an innocent extension into something that could capture and redirect traffic and send data from your sessions.

Exploiting the trusted rating, positive reviews and huge download figures for each extension, the malware has made it's way onto many devices, providing a rich data set for attackers and the potential for massive man-in-the-middle attacks on unsuspecting users.

The advice as always is to check extensions installed in your browser (and remove them if they exist), then clear-out browser data to destroy tracking data and run full malware sweeps of your machine.

Kudos to Koi Security for discovering and disclosing this one.
