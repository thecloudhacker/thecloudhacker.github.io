Cisco Firewall Ongoing Attacks

7th November 2025

Continuing on from the previous Cisco vulnerability in the wild reports, Cisco have uncovered an ongoing attack against Secure ASA Software or Cisco Secure FTD Software releases affected by CVE-2025-20333 and CVE-2025-20362.

The CVE's, which were patched in September after Cisco worked for several months with government agencies to investigate attacks on equipment, can lead to equipment being forced in a reboot cycle. In some cases, the ROMmon has been modified to estblish persistent connections to equipment even after rebooting or software upgrades.

The advice is clear and urgent - patch now. Cisco have more details on their security advisory.

Cisco also notified customers that they have discovered two critical security holes in the contact centre software, tracked as CVE-2025-20354 and CVE-2025-20358. They affect Cisco Unified CCX, regardless of device configuration and allow remote, unauthenticated attackers to upload arbitrary files; execute commands with root privileges; or bypass authentication to run scripts as a non-root user.

In plain terms, if you can't patch your systems, consider the hardware compromised or compromisable. If you have the ability, patch everything right now - turn off features or replace ageing equipment.
