European Airport Check-in System Hacked

21st September 2025

Airlines have been pushed into chaos this weekend with flights delayed due to centralised software, Muse, which is operated by RTX (who owns software provider Collins Aerospace) enduring a "cyber-related disruption".

"The impact is limited to electronic customer check-in and baggage drop and can be mitigated with manual check-in operations." RTX commented. Customers trying to meet connections have found the delays have hindered or cancelled their ongoing journeys.

Heathrow, Dublin and several other airports have had to delay flights and switch to manual check-in processes to try to keep the lines of customers moving. In several airports customers have been left stranded as flights are delayed, rescheduled or cancelled and journeys put on hold.

Other than noting the software affected, Collins Aerospace have yet to comment publicly about the nature or origin of the hack, leaving speculation as to the cause ranging from ransomware to the loss of access to systems.

Given the recent incursion into foreign airspace by Russia this week, there is a heightened nervousness around the origin of this incident.
