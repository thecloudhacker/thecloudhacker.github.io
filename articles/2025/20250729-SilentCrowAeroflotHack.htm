Silent Crow Aeroflot Hack

29th July 2025

The Russian national airline, Aeroflot, has had to cancel more than 40 flights due to a cyberattack on the company's IT systems.

In a statement from the hacking group Silent Crow they claimed responsibility with the help of the Belarusian group Cyber Partisans. Posting on Telegram, Silent Crow stated it's "prolonged and large-scale operation..." left Aeroflot "completely destroyed". They went on to threaten the release of personal details of all Russians who have ever flown with Aeroflot, concluding with a "Glory to Ukraine! Long Live Belarus!".

Silent Crow and Cyber Partisans have been actively engaging in cyber combat since the Russian agression, stating their hacktivist mission as "fighting for the liberation of Belarus from dictatorial rule".

As with many activities behind Russian borders, it's difficult to gain accurate confirmation and assessment of claims due to the regime. How much impact this attack had on the civilian populous and morale are unclear at this time.

In a time when physical and cyber warfare is being engaged, the increase in civilian-based groups fighting back is only guaranteed to increase.
