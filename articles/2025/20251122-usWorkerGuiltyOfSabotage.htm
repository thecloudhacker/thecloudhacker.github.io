U.S. Worker guilty of sabotage on ex-employer

22nd November 2025

A U.S. worker in Ohio has pleaded guilty of sabotaging his ex-employer's computer systems causing $862,000 worth of damage. The 35-year old contractor, Maxwell Shultz, impersonated another contractor to operate powershell scripts and attempt to cover his tracks by removing logs after being fired from the unnamed company.

The scripts reset the passwords to around 2,500 accounts on the system and caused the stated damage cost in downtime, disruption to customer services and remediation.

Maxwell will be sentenced at the end of January, facing up to ten years in prison and up to $250,000 in fines.

Yet another case of disgruntled employees with too much access to systems highlights the need to have sanitation routines on passwords, keys and systems when offboarding employees with wide-ranging access.
