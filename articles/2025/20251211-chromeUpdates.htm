Another Chrome "Patch Now" Drop

11th December 2025

Google have pushed security updates for the Chrome browser to fix three security flaws with one being found under active exploitation. The team are keeping the details under close guard until enough of the userbase has upgraded to the latest patched versions.

"Access to bug details and links may be kept restricted until a majority of users are updated with a fix. We will also retain restrictions if the bug exists in a third party library that other projects similarly depend on, but havenâ€™t yet fixed."

The update 143.0.7499.109/.110 on Windows or Mac and 143.0.7499.109 on Linux are the current patched versions. It's important to ensure you run the update as soon as possible.

This update takes the quantity of zero-day flaws to eight this year. Thankfully the security teams are actively engaged in seeking and resolving issues before they reach the stable channel. The latest updates continue to prove that continuous patching cycles are necessary to maintain a strong security posture.
