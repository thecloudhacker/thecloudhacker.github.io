KLM and Air France Targeted

8th August 2025

KLM and Air France have become the latest in a series of air travel companies to be targeted by cybercriminal groups attempting to access and exfiltrate critical data.

The airlines who are part of the same group detected unusual activity on an external platform utilised by KLM and Air France customer service. This appears to have led to unauthorised access to customer data. The security teams acted quickly to stop the unauthorised access however the firms have not publicly specify the types of data stolen, other than citing "No sensitive data, such as passwords, travel details, Flying Blue miles, passport or credit card information was stolen".

It would appear that firstname, surname and contact details have been disclosed in the breach from shared images of documents sent to customers.

The airlines have advised customers to be on the alert for phishing attempts using the limited details acquired from the customer system.

It's not known as yet who the purpetrator of the incident was, however it has all the hallmarks of the ShinyHunters group.
