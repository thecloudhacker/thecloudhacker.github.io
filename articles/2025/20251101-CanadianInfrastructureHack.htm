Canadian Infrastructure Hack

1st November 2025

The Canadian Centre for Cyber Security issued an alert to raise awareness of a cyber threat impacting critical infrastructure across Canada.

Hacktivists have managed to breach municipal water facility systems along with an Oil and Gas company. In an effort to raise awareness of their own cause, the Hacktivists tampered with settings and guage readings at facilities, thankfully not causing major incidents or injury/death.

The Canadian Centre for Cyber Security warns that the attack highlights the failings to secure critical national infrastructure, with the usual suspects of Internet connected systems that are vulnerable, open, or not adequately patched. PLC's, remote terminal units, human-machine interfaces, SCADA systems, safety controllers, building management setups and other IoT equipment left exposed and open to tamper from anyone on the Internet.

Companies providing critical infrastructure and services must take stock of their systems and access points into the equipment they operate, or the next time it might not be a Hacktivist group raising awareness, but a nation state taking-out adversaries.
