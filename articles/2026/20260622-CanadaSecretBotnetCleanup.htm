Canada Secretly Cleans Botnet-Infected Devices

22nd June 2026

The Canadian Security Intelligence Service was given a warrant for threat reduction measures in a first-of-it's-kind ruling allowing them to "alter, degrade, and destroy botnet data on the infected machines and cut the devices loose from the networks". In other words, hack into devices, remove the crud and patch the infrastructure without the owner's intervention.

Involved in the Botnet were Canada-based servers, small office and home office routers, Internet-of-things devices, Ring doorbells, Security Cameras, TV's and Wifi-enabled devices.

Whilst the motive of preventing loss, theft of details, denial of service over services and further illegal activity, the modification of services and hardware with persistent fixes presents an ethical conundrum. Canada's court found the threat clearly established and imminent with the measures deemed necessary, reasonable and proportional. Do the end users and customers, however?

Reaching into a device that is not your own and wiping data is classified as computer mischief under the Criminal Code, hence the required court ruling before undertaking the work. It is not clear, however, how the agency collected information leading up to the request.

Should the customers have been notified first or in the process? Answers on a postcard...
