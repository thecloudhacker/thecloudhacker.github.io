Romanian Land Registry Hack

22nd July 2026

On Tuesday 14th July, the Romanian cadastre application, known as e-Terra, and internal email systems went offline. This very quickly led the Romania's real-estate market grinding to a halt as notaries could not authenticate sales, register mortgages, or it's citizens pull proof of ownership.

Romania's National Cyber Security Directorate (DNSC) has confirmed the attack was not a complex campaign and would have been prevented with some simple steps:
Known, unpatched vulnerabilities (that, according to the DNSC, had recently flagged to the Land Registry), combined with leaked credentials that were posted online, and a Group Policy named "DISABLE WINDOWS FIREWALL" led to the compromise. There was no zero-day, and the attacker did not need any form of bypass.

By Wednesday, stolen data was being offered for sale online in hacking forums and information surrounding the environment at the Land Registry was being offered-up.

ThreatLocker has a full write-up of the incident, highlighting how even simple, traditional methods can lead to disasterous implications without the need for APT-level technology to bring national services to a halt.

If ever there was a call to review your estate, policy configuration and vulnerability management programme, this is it.
