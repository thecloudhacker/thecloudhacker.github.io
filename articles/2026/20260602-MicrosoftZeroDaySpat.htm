Microsoft spat with 0-day hunter continues

2nd June 2026

The ongoing spat between Microsoft and Nightmare Eclipse (aka Chaotic Eclipse) seems to be moving into damage control mode. With the feud spilling into security channels and warnings that major 0-day vulnerabilities will be disclosed in July, Microsoft now seems to be attempting to save face in the security research ecosystem.

With Nightmare-Eclipse booted from Github, kicked-out of legitimate channels and having payments withheld before being hung out and publicly accused of violating CVD, Microsoft finally seems to be reading the room as researchers consider their own future in disclosing major vulnerabilities with Microsoft products.

In a statement, Microsoft said it had "no intention to pursue action against individuals conducting or publishing security research", which is in stark contrast to threatening language days earlier in condemning public disclosure and warning it would work with law enforcement when criminal activity harmed customers.

Responsible disclosure with financial rewards is a valuable practice for businesses, but the vilification of those researchers following your processes and reneging on the deal will only lead to the possibility vulnerabilities will be sold to, shall we say, the more financially forthcoming and less ethically inclined channels.

We will have to see if there are further issues arising from the incident as the community looks on and watches unpatched vulnerabilities dropping into the public domain.
