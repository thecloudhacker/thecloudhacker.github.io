Indian Government suffers sustained campaign

11th February 2026

India's defence sector and government-aligned organisations are suffering an ongoing series of campaigns targeting Windows and Linux systems with malware in the form of Remote Access Tooling.

The findings by Aryaka uncovered a range of attacks using proven techniques to infiltrate systems through malware-laiden documents and links in emails.

The sustained campaigns against India at the start of this year show a refining of compromise mechanisms by the threat actors, designed for longer-term access of the target systems. The use of malware families Geta RAT, Ares RAT, and DeskRAT are often attributed to Pakistan-aligned threat groups tracked as SideCopy and APT36.

Aryaka states: "These campaigns demonstrate a well-resourced, espionage-focused threat actor deliberately targeting Indian defense, government, and strategic sectors through defense-themed lures, impersonated official documents, and regionally trusted infrastructure. The activity extends beyond defense to policy, research, critical infrastructure, and defense-adjacent organizations operating within the same trusted ecosystem"
