Checkmarx KICS compromised

22nd April 2026

Another day, another supply chain compromise, but this time within tooling used by vulnerability scanning. The Checkmarx KICS dockerhub repository was compromised with malicious images that overwrote existing tags. Uncovered by the team at Socket, the analysis shows the binary was modified to include data collection and exfiltration routines.

The evidence gathered showed uncensored scan reports were generated, encrypted and sent to an external address. This could potentially lead to the exfiltration of passwords or secrets and sensitive vulnerability information being utilised by attackers without the knowledge of the business utilising the scanning software.

KICS is utilised in a range of companies and in systems such as Datadog's IaC scanning software, leading to the potential for wider-scale compromise of businesses utilising their software. The new normal, it would appear, is that everywhere is being hit with supply chain attacks to work towards targets.

As always, keep an eye on the things that are watching things. I'm sure there's an old Soviet adage that could be employed here...
