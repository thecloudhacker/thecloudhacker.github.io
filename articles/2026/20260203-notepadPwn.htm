Notepad++ servers used to deliver Malware

3rd February 2026

The popular editor software, Notepad++, has suffered a targeted compromise on the hosting platform that delivered updates for their software by ( with high confidence from Rapid7 ) China-linked APT group "Lotus Blossom".

Beginning in June 2025, the compromise allowed the threat actors to selectively redirect requests from certain users to malicious servers to serve a tampered update by exploiting insufficient update verification controls that existed in older versions of the utility.

The team at Notepad++ have stated the incident was closed by December 2nd with a change of hosting infrastructure and hardening of the security practices in the software.

The recommendation is to download and install v8.9.1 to ensure no hijack of the update system.
